The Festival is over and here is our Waves Music Festival 2015 - Vienna and Bratislava recap. It was nice to see some fresh music again. We found some new cool acts even tough we didn't manage to watch as many as last year. A big surprise was definitely the super good quality of venues and acts we saw in Bratislava. It could be just the fresh impression of a new city but still I would say it was much better than Vienna. The crowds were better, the locations were better and the soundsystem (+ soundguys) were better. So I am not quite sure what is happening with the Waves Vienna cause in my opinion the overall quality on the Viennese side of the festival is constantly dropping over the years whereas Bratislava is constantly rising.

But again we are very picky and complaining about everything cause we have been in Vienna too long, haha.

## Waves Bratislava 2015
Finally this year we made it to the third edition of the [WAVES Bratislava](http://www.wavescentraleurope.com/category/wavesbratislava-en/) and still we were sad we only made it on the last night, because it was the bomb! Via bus we went from the WAVES Vienna's festival centre at Alte Post to Bratislava, bus only takes an hour and leaves each hour in both directions. With the festival pass for both cities the ride is for free.In Bratislava we saw [Jonathan](https://youtu.be/sqiWBVJFXos?t=2m29s) again. Our favorite band from last year [(read more here)](https://www.ottersandweasels.com/waves14-day-1/). Still amazing. Somebody should book them to Vienna. Fismoll was quite stunning especially playing in the old theatre -> MDPOH venue. And the big hall Stara Trznica was super impressive as a venue too. There we watched a bit of the rapper Supa, who was quite good and got the crowd going. At the polish Institute Bratislava we also caught a few minutes of Stroon, who makes some amazing electronica combined with his big xylophone. A big advantage is the proximity of all the venues, within 5 minutes you can reach them all and are almost along one street, which is super handy if you wanna see as much concerts as possible. We were surprised by the originality of the clubs/venues, which covered underground pubs, fancy exhibition spaces up to old office buildings going up to the 4th floor in an early 90ies elevator to reach a club is always a good indicator.

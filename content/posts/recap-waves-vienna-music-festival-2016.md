This year's [Waves Vienna Music Festival](http://www.wavescentraleurope.com/?lang=de) took place in the 9th city district of Vienna. Offering a variety of locations that were all in walking distance and very well chosen.
The [WUK](http://www.wuk.at/) formed the headquarters with it's legendary Beisl this location has been a cultural institution in Vienna for decades supporting artists and social encounters.

One of the unusual locations was the Show Room located opposite the WUK featuring the daily warm up and new fresh bands. The concerts were titled shop window concerts. The artists perform in the shop window and the crowd is listening on head phones, like a silent disco.

One of this year's focus "all eyes on" country was Israel. The cultural highly diverse country is also combining a huge variety of influences in the music scene, which could be seen by the highly professional and fresh bands. One of them was Lola Marsh.

The second "all eyes on" country was Germany. Here the loud and dirty bands were the majority. Heating up the crowd with a clever mix of virtuosity, show and fun. One of our favorites and nice surprises was the band Golf.

One of our favorite locations were the Schubert Theater and the Palme, hidden downstairs in an old Viennese coffeehouse featuring the DJ-line and afterparty.

## [Milk+](http://www.milkplus.net/)
perfectionism and true mastership of their instruments. really a pleasure to watch also they didn't shy away from playing the whole set in one piece like a story. They remind me strongly of The Mars Volta in their perfectionism and dedication towards their music, funny that their last album was produced by Mars Volta's Ikey Owens.
## [Ephemerals](http://www.ephemeralsmusic.com/)
 
## [Hannah Epperson](http://www.hannahepperson.ca/)
was the highlight of the festival by far. I have never heard of her and just thought violin sounds good and the puppet theatre was my favorite location anyway, so why not watch this. And I was blown away, like all the other people who witnessed this amazing concert.

The person Hannah Epperson is already amazing when she just talkes some gibberish nonsense or also some clever stuff. Everything she says is just really charming and amusing :) AND THEN she even makes such wonderful music and soundscape with her jazzy drummer. Big big big recommendation. Watch her whenever you see that she is coming to a town near you. You will not regret it.

## We are Scientists
They were more or less the headliners and played the last show on the last day of the festival but something was really off. The sound was super crappy and it is always hard to say if the sound engineer was that bad or some other technical difficulties were the problem, but in the end it sounded like Shit. I was quite a big fan back in the days and was looking forward to see my old heroes but I had to leave after half of the show and could not take it anymore. For the end I went in once more trying to see if it has changed, but it hadn't and the very mild applause at the end of the show said more that 1000 words.

 

 

 

We truly missed Zak Abel cause at some point he was on the list of acts. Unfortunately also Gold Panda und Juicy Gay cancelled, which were also 2 acts we would have loved to see there.
